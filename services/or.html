SELECT DOCUMENT:

REQUIREMENTS:

  • Recent Cedula (Community Tax Certificate)
  • Application Form
  • Application Fee (price vary depending on the Barangay)
  • Valid ID

PROCEDURES:

STEP 1:

Go to the Barangay Hall or Municipal Office and tell the officer you will need a Barangay Clearance. They will ask you for what purpose you will use it so you must tell them the reasons – employment, ID requirement, Police Clearance requirement, Postal ID, Affidavits, etc.

STEP 2:

Provide your Community Tax Certificate (Cedula). If you don’t have one yet, you can just get one at the Barangay office, too. Note that each community tax certificate charges a specific fee for each applicant.

STEP 3:

Pay the necessary fee for the issuance of your Barangay Clearance. Keep the receipt for the claiming of your certificate.

STEP 4:

Claim your clearance. They will usually release it in a few minutes depending on the number of applicants and documentations at the Office of the Barangay Captain.

REQUIREMENTS:

  • Proof that you are a Filipino citizen; birth certificate
  • Proof of residency via census; you can use a document like a billing statement
  • 1×1 or 2×2 picture; shot for ID purpose (formal picture)
  • Certificate of employment from the last employer (if you work in this barangay)

PROCEDURES:

STEP 1:

Go to your Barangay Hall

STEP 2:

Look for the Barangay Captain or any assigned councilor for the day

STEP 3:

Fill out the application form

STEP 4:

Submit your documents

STEP 5:

Pay the fee if there is any

STEP 6:

Wait for further instructions how to claim the ID

REQUIREMENTS:

  • Fire Safety Inspection Certificate
  • Sanitary Permit
  • Building Permit and Electrical Inspection Certificate
  • Certificate of Occupancy
  • Locational Clearance
  • Electric Utility Connection

PROCEDURES:

STEP 1:

Go to the Business Permit and Licensing Office (BPLO) within the premises of the city/municipal hall having jurisdiction over your business address. Alternatively, you may conveniently apply for a new business permit or renewal at off-site locations in malls (such as SM, Robinsons, Ayala, and Vista Malls) that have partnerships with your LGU.

STEP 2:

Secure and fill out the application form. The form is available on-site, or you can download it in advance from the LGU's website.

STEP 3:

Submit the accomplished application form and other requirements. The personnel will evaluate your documents. Your application will also undergo an assessment of tax and fees.

STEP 4:

Pay for the assessed tax and fees to the cashier (once approved). Receive your official receipt.

STEP 5:

Claim your business permit. The document may be issued together with a registration plate/sticker or any other certificate. Make sure to display them prominently in your store or office.

REQUIREMENTS:

  • Barangay Attestation / Barangay Certificate of Residency
  • Credentials out No Property starting Municipal Assessor's Office
  • Certificate of No Business upon the Municipal Treasury Office
  • Certificate of Tax Exemption from the Secretariat of Internal Revenue (BIR)
  • Valuation Report from to CSWDO or MSWDO
