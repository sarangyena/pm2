ABOUT US

Legendarily, a hundred years ago during the Spanish Colonization, a Spanish soldier was walking and looking for someone. He passed a vast and grassy road and asked one bystander if what wast the name of the barrio. The man answered "this is KARSADA" pointing to the area where the only road links from Tipas to Sta Ana which later on was called CALZADA. The people that lived in the district of Tipas were from the clans of people who were almost related to each other like Tanyag, Maglipon, Dimaguila, Sulit, Isagon, Ysagun, Estacion, Herrera, Juta, Pagkalinawan, Sta. Barbara, Carlos, Reyes, Bonifacio, Gregorio, Bunyi, Mabilin, Sarmiento, Garcia, Bernabe, Gabriel, Flores, Magsalin, Malanay, Sañga and Ordoñez. Baragay Calzada is one of the twenty eight (28) barangays of Taguig City located in the First (1st) District of Lower Taguig. In 1972, under the declaration of Martial Law, President Ferdinand Marcos then sign a Presidential Decree No.557 that Tipas was subdivided into four (4) barangays and were named as Barangay Calzada-Tipas, Barangay Palingon-Tipas, Barangay Ligid-Tipas and Barangay Ibayo-Tipas.
