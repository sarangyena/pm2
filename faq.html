FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs)

Your go-to resource for answers to commonly asked questions. This collection of frequently asked questions provides you with quick and concise information on a variety of topics. From general inquiries to specific details, these FAQs cover a wide range of subjects to address your concerns. Whether you're looking for contact information, seeking clarification on services, or curious about processes and procedures, our FAQs are designed to offer clear and helpful responses. Save time and find the answers you need by browsing through these frequently asked questions.

WHAT IS A BARANGAY?

A barangay is the smallest administrative division in the Philippines, often referred to as a village or neighborhood. It serves as the primary local government unit responsible for delivering basic services and promoting the welfare of its residents.

WHAT ARE THE RESPONSIBILITIES OF A BARANGAY?

Barangays have various responsibilities, including maintaining peace and order, providing basic healthcare services, ensuring the cleanliness of the community, promoting education, organizing local events, issuing barangay clearances, and facilitating community development projects, among others.

HOW CAN I CONTACT OUR BARANGAY OFFICIALS?

You can typically contact your barangay officials by visiting the barangay hall, attending community meetings, or checking their contact information on the official barangay website or bulletin boards. In some cases, you may also reach out to them through phone or email.

WHAT SERVICES CAN I AVAIL FROM THE BARANGAY?

Barangays offer a range of services, such as assistance for community events, barangay clearances, issuance of permits, waste management programs, assistance for indigent residents, street lighting, maintenance of local facilities (like parks and basketball courts), and mediation of disputes within the community.

HOW CAN I PARTICIPATE IN BARANGAY ACTIVITIES AND PROGRAMS?

To participate in barangay activities and programs, you can attend community meetings, volunteer for local projects, join community organizations or committees, and engage with barangay officials. Stay updated with announcements through the barangay's communication channels, such as social media platforms, newsletters, or local bulletin boards.

HOW CAN I REPORT A PROBLEM OR CONCERT TO THE BARANGAY?

You can report any problem or concern to the barangay by visiting the barangay hall in person, contacting the barangay officials via phone or email, or submitting a written complaint or request. Provide as much detail as possible to help them address the issue effectively.

HOW ARE BARANGAY OFFICIALS ELECTED?

Barangay officials are elected through democratic elections held every three years. The election process involves eligible residents casting their votes for candidates running for various positions, including barangay captain (chairperson), councilors, and other appointed officials.

HOW CAN I OBTAIN A BARANGAY CLEARANCE?

To obtain a barangay clearance, visit the barangay hall and inquire about the requirements and procedures. Typically, you'll need to provide identification documents, proof of residence, and any specific documents required for the purpose of obtaining the clearance (e.g., for employment, business, or travel purposes).

HOW ARE BARANGAYS FUNDED?

Barangays receive funding from the national government as part of their internal revenue allotment (IRA) based on specific formulas. Additionally, they may generate revenue through local taxes, fees, and donations. These funds are utilized for the delivery of basic services and the implementation of local projects.

CAN I PROPOSE A PROJECT OR INITIATIVE FOR OUR BARANGAY?

Yes, you can propose a project or initiative for your barangay. Start by discussing your ideas with the barangay officials or attending community meetings to present your proposal. They can guide you through the necessary steps, requirements, and processes to help bring your project to fruition.
